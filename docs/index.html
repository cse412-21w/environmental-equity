Project Abstract

The focus of our final project is environmental equity. As we look at ways to address the global warming crisis, there is a struggle between scientists pushing for fast and decisive action to save the planet, and policy makers looking to balance short term needs like employment and economic growth. Green alternatives are unfortunately not always the most economical and the economic costs often disproportionately affect more vulnerable groups. Developing countries often bear more of the burden of climate change and are less equipped to adapt. Our project will aim to answer the following questions: What groups are currently impacted by the impacts of global warming, and what groups stand to be impacted most in the coming years? What climate change solutions can equitably and positvely affect all groups? How can the burden of economic impacts of these solutions be equitably distributed?

Global Analysis via the GAIN Index

Quick description: This visualization comes from data provided by Notre Dame's Global Adaptation Initiative Index. This index aims to assess countries' exposure to climate change as well as their ability to adapt. The map on the left shows the Vulnerability Score for each country, while the map on the right shows the Readiness Score. Vulnerability refers to the potential for negative impact due to climate change, while Readiness encompasses a country's capacity to leverage new technologies to take adaptive action. These scores are calculated using a multitude of indicators across various sectors: health, food, water, governance, energy, technology, economy, and so on. I chose a map to dispaly this data as it is powerful to see how geography plays a role in risk and readiness. By comparing between the two maps, we can see that many of the countries who are the most vulnerable to climate change are also the least ready to combat it--emphasizing the environmental inequity that exists between developed and developing countries, and how critical it is that fighting climate change is a worldwide effort.

Quick description: This visualization dives deeper into the data displayed above. The data is broken up into four quadrants based on Climate Vulnerability and Readiness. A negative sloping trend is apparent, showcasing that the countrie at the highest risk are also the least prepared. Quick Description: There are a number of concerning metrics when it comes to equity and climate vulnerability. Visualized here are several key metrics: Vulnerability Score (), Readiness Score, and Poverty Level (calculated based on percent of a nation's citizens living below a baseline world income level). The two concerning trends are quite obvious, yet unsurprising. Countries with a higher percentage of the population below the poverty level are far more vulnerable to factors associated with climate change and are far less prepared for climate change. On the left hand graph, points are also color-coded based on their readiness score, emphasizing how nations with high levels of poverty are both more vulnerable and less prepared.
